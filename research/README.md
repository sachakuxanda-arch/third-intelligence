# Research

This folder is the part of Third Intelligence where reality gets its vote recorded.

Most of the field is focused on making AI more capable. This work focuses on the other side of the relationship: whether the human capacity to keep learning — to close loops, calibrate, and compound understanding — can be treated as trainable, measurable infrastructure. That position is unlikely to be popular before it is evidenced. This folder is where the evidence (for or against) accumulates.

## Contents

| File | What it is |
|---|---|
| [`predictions.md`](predictions.md) | The ledger. Registered, measurable predictions with status. Wording is frozen at registration. |
| [`critique-log.md`](critique-log.md) | Objections received, the reply, and what changed. Seeded with the strongest objections we know of. |
| [`evidence/`](evidence/) | Documented cases of the Apparency Error and, over time, data generated by the environments. |

## Operating rules

1. **Registered wording is frozen.** A prediction's claim and measure do not change after registration. If a measure proves wrong, the prediction is closed (`WITHDRAWN`, with reasons) and a successor is registered with a new ID.
2. **Status moves on evidence, not argument.** `OPEN → SUPPORTED / FALSIFIED / WITHDRAWN`. A persuasive essay changes nothing here; data does.
3. **Critiques are logged whether or not they are answered well.** An objection that currently wins stays in the log marked `OPEN — UNANSWERED`. That is the point.
4. **Failures stay on the record.** Nothing is quietly deleted from this folder.

## Contributing

The goal is not agreement. The goal is better models. Submit critiques via the [contact page](https://sachakuxanda-arch.github.io/third-intelligence/contact.html?type=critique) or open an issue. The strongest version of the objection is the most useful one.
