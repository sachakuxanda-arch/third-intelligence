# Evidence — Documented Cases of the Apparency Error

**The claim these cases support, stated precisely:** the pattern of apparent alignment diverging from actual alignment exists, recurs across unrelated domains, and is expensive. **The claim they do not support:** that ΔE is currently measurable, or that upstream intervention is proven tractable. (See critique C-004 — this scoping exists because of it.)

These are starting summaries to be expanded with full sourcing. Each case should eventually carry: primary sources, the specific divergence (what appeared agreed vs. what was actually understood), the point at which the gap surfaced, and documented cost.

---

## Lidl / SAP (2011–2018)

A seven-year ERP implementation abandoned after approximately €500M. The widely reported core failure: the business operated on inventory valued at purchase price, the system assumed retail price — a divergence in foundational models between business and technical teams that persisted beneath years of apparent project alignment until execution made it undeniable.

**Pattern:** divergent base assumptions under sustained apparent agreement; surfaced only at execution scale.

## Boeing 737 MAX (2018–2019)

346 deaths across two crashes. Investigations (House Committee report, 2020; JATR, 2019) documented divergent understandings of MCAS — its authority, its failure modes, what pilots needed to know — operating across engineering, certification, and commercial functions under apparent consensus that the aircraft was safe and the differences immaterial.

**Pattern:** divergent risk models under apparent institutional consensus; the gap surfaced catastrophically rather than procedurally.

## Enron (2001)

Documented extensively (Powers Report, 2002; McLean & Elkind). Relevant to this frame: layers of apparent agreement — between board and management, auditors and accounts, stated controls and actual practice — in which many participants believed alignment existed because the artifacts of alignment (approvals, sign-offs, audited statements) existed.

**Pattern:** artifacts of agreement mistaken for agreement itself.

## The Abilene Paradox (Harvey, 1974)

The behavioural naming of the mechanism: a group collectively decides on an action that no individual member wants, because each privately dissents while publicly inferring consensus. Harvey's contribution is that the failure is not disagreement but *mismanaged agreement* — the gap between expressed and actual positions.

**Pattern:** the mechanism in miniature, documented for fifty years.
